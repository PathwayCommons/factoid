Factoid demo

Enter abstract

Enter your paper's abstract in the area to the right. Factoid will find biological entities and interactions in your abstract and add them to the view on the left.

Note: We initially guide you through Factoid in steps, but you can use any part of Factoid in any order.

Choose organisms

Factoid has automatically found organisms relevant to your abstract, but you may want to manually adjust them above. Selected organisms are used to refine searches.

Note: We initially guide you through Factoid in steps, but you can use any part of Factoid in any order.

Refine entities & interactions

Now that your network document is seeded based on your abstract and chosen organisms, you can refine your list of biological entities (e.g. proteins) and interactions:
  • Confirm the identity of each entity, indicated by a checkmark to the left of its name.
  • Confirm the list of participants for each interaction.
  • Add missing and remove erroneous entities and interactions related to your paper.

Factoid: Building the future of scientific publishing

Factoid helps authors translate their written scientific text into formal descriptions of biological processes. This is useful for sharing results with others, bioinformatics analysis and integrating with other data to help build a more complete model of a cell.

Factoid aims to be part of the publication process. With the author's help, it will extract pathway and interaction information from a paper, produce a network view as a visual and interactive abstract and publish the result in a shareable and computable format.

Factoid 1.0 helps turn text into a simple and editable network model of a biological process. Text is automatically converted to a first draft network which can then be corrected using the Factoid editor. We plan to improve the amount, accuracy and detail of the information Factoid can automatically find in a paper in future software versions.
